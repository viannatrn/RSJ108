How to Work for National Geographic

Completion Time:1 hour 30 minutes
Effectiveness Rating: 100% Effective

National Geographic is a global community of journalists, photographers, filmmakers, and scientists that entertain and educate the community through magazines and television channels. Are you interested in becoming a part of this community? With a bit of research and lots of hard work and dedication, you can begin the fascinating journey of working for National Geographic by channeling your passion to create and explore.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Electronic device (preferably a computer/laptop)
  • Wifi access
  • Attentive research
  • College degree in either science, journalism, sociology, anthropology, fine arts, or some related discipline
  • Love for adventure
  • Passion for storytelling

  1. Choose your focus

    In the case of either journalistic writng or photojournalism, search for job listings under the National Geographic Society and look into the ones that peak your interest. Be realistic and research the dangers of each job as well. Take note of the requirements for each job and ask yourself how your current experience ties into each of them. It's important to remember that National Geographic values passion and exploration in addition to academic background.

  2. Obtain the necessary experience

    Whether you want to be a writer, photographer, or explorer, nature expeditions are a great way to get both inspiration and experience. If you want a photography career at National Geographic, get some experience in professional photojournalism in a specialty of your interest. Apply to as many different publications as you can, even if it's just freelance work, in order to build relevant writing experience. You may also send in your story ideas and links to your work to editorial@natgeotraveller.co.uk.

  3. Develop your skills to focus on storytelling, research, and conservation.

    Take your education and interests and channel them into desirable National Geographic skills. Regularly brainstorm new projects. Get in the habit of constantly asking yourself how your unique work and skills can tell an innovative story about the world around you. National Geographic values unique experience more than anything, so don't cater your education to a job in National Geographic—do something you enjoy! As you progress through your degree, ask yourself how your learning can be applied to projects that would be beneficial for National Geographic.

  4. Finding National Geographic Jobs

    Check jobs from National Geographic Partners for various media roles. Whether you're interested in photography or journalism, National Geographic Partners offers jobs in their television and production arm. As mentioned previously, National Geographic Society has a more varied array of job listings. So keep an eye out for jobs that match your experience, education, and skills. For undergraduate and graduate students, National Geographic offers summer internships to gain experience and job-related skills. Look for open positions on their website in January and apply via the online application. It may be useful to know that explorers in the fields of research, education, conservation, and technology are eligible for a grant. National Geographic prefers projects that are not just strictly research work and integrate experiences outside of the laboratory. In addition, it must fit into one of three categories: Wildlife, the Human Journey, or the Changing Planet.

  5. Applying for a Job

    Create your own resume and cover letter. Include all of your relevant employment and educational history in your resume. Be sure to list all of your special skills and qualifications—National Geographic values these highly. When it comes to personal information such as values and the things that drive and motivate you, save them for your cover letter. Create an account on the National Geographic job listing site. Both National Geographic Partners and National Geographic Society operate via the Workday database. The database allows members to apply for National Geographic jobs via their server. To use it, you need to create an account by providing your email and a password. From here, you can upload your resume, cover letter, and experience. Once you have your resume and cover letter, visit the job listing that interests you and submit an application. Due to the competition for National Geographic jobs, expect to wait at least 6 months to hear back for acceptance.
